Creamy Garlic Parmesan Pasta

Prep Time
10 minutes
Cook Time
20 minutes
Servings
4
Difficulty
Easy

Ingredients

  • 8 oz fettuccine pasta
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook pasta according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. In a large skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Pour in heavy cream and bring to a simmer. Cook for 3-4 minutes until it begins to thicken slightly.
  4. Reduce heat to low and gradually whisk in Parmesan cheese until melted and smooth.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Add cooked pasta to the sauce and toss to coat. If sauce is too thick, add a splash of reserved pasta water.
  7. Garnish with chopped parsley and red pepper flakes if desired.
  8. Serve immediately with additional Parmesan cheese on top.

Decadent Chocolate Cake

Prep Time
25 minutes
Cook Time
35 minutes
Servings
12
Difficulty
Medium

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup hot coffee
  • For frosting: 1/2 cup butter, 2/3 cup cocoa powder, 3 cups powdered sugar, 1/3 cup milk, 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Add eggs, buttermilk, oil, and vanilla. Beat with an electric mixer on medium speed for 2 minutes.
  4. Stir in hot coffee (batter will be thin). Pour batter evenly into prepared pans.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in center comes out clean.
  6. Cool in pans for 10 minutes, then remove to wire racks to cool completely.
  7. For frosting: Melt butter and stir in cocoa powder. Alternately add powdered sugar and milk, beating to spreading consistency. Add vanilla and mix well.
  8. Spread frosting between layers and on top and sides of cake.
  9. Let cake stand for 1 hour before serving.

Avocado Toast with Poached Egg

Prep Time
10 minutes
Cook Time
5 minutes
Servings
2
Difficulty
Easy

Ingredients

  • 2 slices of sourdough bread
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • 1/2 lemon, juiced
  • Red pepper flakes
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh herbs (chives, cilantro, or parsley)
  • Extra virgin olive oil for drizzling

Instructions

  1. Toast the bread slices until golden and crisp.
  2. Cut the avocado in half, remove the pit, and scoop the flesh into a bowl.
  3. Add lemon juice, salt, and pepper to the avocado and mash with a fork, leaving some chunks for texture.
  4. For the poached eggs: Bring a pot of water to a gentle simmer and add vinegar.
  5. Crack each egg into a small bowl, then gently slide into the simmering water.
  6. Cook for 3-4 minutes until whites are set but yolks are still runny.
  7. Remove eggs with a slotted spoon and place on a paper towel to drain excess water.
  8. Spread the mashed avocado evenly on the toast slices.
  9. Top each toast with a poached egg, sprinkle with red pepper flakes and fresh herbs.
  10. Drizzle with olive oil and add additional salt and pepper if desired.
  11. Serve immediately.

Traditional Korean Bibimbap

Prep Time
30 minutes
Cook Time
20 minutes
Servings
4
Difficulty
Medium

Ingredients

  • 4 cups cooked short-grain rice
  • 1/2 lb beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup spinach, blanched and seasoned
  • 1 cup bean sprouts, blanched and seasoned
  • 1 carrot, julienned and sautéed
  • 1 zucchini, julienned and sautéed
  • 1 cup shiitake mushrooms, sliced and sautéed
  • 4 eggs, fried
  • 4 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions

  1. Marinate beef in soy sauce, sesame oil, brown sugar, and minced garlic for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Cook each vegetable separately: blanch spinach and bean sprouts, then season with salt and sesame oil; sauté carrots, zucchini, and mushrooms with a pinch of salt.
  3. Cook the marinated beef in a hot pan until browned and cooked through.
  4. Fry eggs sunny-side up, keeping the yolks runny.
  5. To assemble: place warm rice in four bowls.
  6. Arrange the beef and vegetables in separate sections on top of the rice.
  7. Place a fried egg in the center of each bowl.
  8. Serve with gochujang on the side or directly on top.
  9. Before eating, mix everything together thoroughly.
  10. Garnish with sesame seeds and serve immediately.

Fluffy Pancakes with Fresh Berries

Prep Time
15 minutes
Cook Time
10 minutes
Servings
4
Difficulty
Easy

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 3/4 cups milk
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups mixed fresh berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • Maple syrup for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, beat the eggs, then add milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Don't overmix; a few lumps are okay.
  4. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or frying pan over medium-high heat.
  5. Pour or scoop the batter onto the griddle, using approximately 1/4 cup for each pancake.
  6. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges look dry, about 2-3 minutes.
  7. Flip and cook until browned on the other side, about 1-2 minutes more.
  8. Serve hot with fresh berries and maple syrup.

Grilled Chicken Salad

Prep Time
20 minutes
Cook Time
15 minutes
Servings
2
Difficulty
Medium

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried herbs (thyme, oregano, rosemary)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 6 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds
  • For the dressing: 3 tablespoons olive oil, 1 tablespoon lemon juice, 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard, 1 clove garlic (minced), salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush chicken breasts with olive oil and season with dried herbs, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill chicken for 6-7 minutes per side, or until internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  4. Let chicken rest for 5 minutes, then slice into strips.
  5. In a large bowl, combine salad greens, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and red onion.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together all dressing ingredients.
  7. Toss the salad with the dressing.
  8. Top with grilled chicken strips, feta cheese, and sliced almonds.
  9. Serve immediately.
